A silver mining thesis, published and graded in public.

In November 2024 I co-wrote a buy case on First Majestic Silver (NYSE: AG) for a university course. This site grades that call against what actually happened, runs a comparables dashboard that updates itself daily, and hosts my current research. I use AI tools for the code and the data plumbing. The analysis and the calls are mine.

The November 2024 call, graded

Seven specific predictions from the original analysis (Nov 21, 2024, AG at US$6.42), each graded 19 months later. Some were right for the wrong reason, and honestly the misses taught me more than the wins.

Prediction (Nov 2024)GradeWhat actually happened
First Majestic is undervalued, a buying opportunity Right on direction +171% since the call. But our multiples model implied only ~6% upside. It couldn't see the silver price itself, which is why the current research uses a NAV model instead.
Jerritt Canyon will be sold or reopened as a more efficient mine, 2026 to 2027 Right, best call Restart plan announced April 2026: US$75M invested in 2026, pre-feasibility study due Q4 2026, production targeted H2 2027.
Gatos acquisition strains liquidity short-term; leverage then improves Half right Right outcome, wrong mechanism: the deal was all-stock (~190M new shares), so the cost was dilution, not debt. Debt/EBITDA fell from 2.1x to 0.5x as EBITDA scaled.
ROE recovers via margin repair (DuPont: net margin was the broken lever) Right From a US$182M net loss in 2023 to record profits. Q1 2026 alone: US$477M revenue, US$128M net earnings.
First Mint vertical integration boosts margins Right, small so far Q4 2025 mint revenue +150% YoY to US$22.7M; ISO 9001 certified, IRA-eligible. Still ~2% of revenue.
Green-technology demand supports silver long-term Right thesis, different trigger Silver roughly doubled, but the spike came from a physical shortage running into a wave of Chinese retail buying. Industrial demand was the backdrop, not the trigger.
AG breaks away from peers on costs via economies of scale Not yet proven AISC held flat at US$21.17/AgEq oz through a major integration. Respectable, but not a breakaway. Tracked in the dashboard below.
The uncomfortable footnote: this was a sector-wide rally. AG beat silver and half its peer group, but the biggest winners were the lowest-cost producer (Fresnillo) and small names with high operating leverage. Being bullish on silver and picking the best silver miner are different skills. The current research below is built around that lesson.
