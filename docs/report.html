Investment thesis

First Majestic today is a much better business than the one we recommended in November 2024. Production hit a record 31.1M AgEq oz in 2025. The balance sheet went from 2.1x debt/EBITDA to a US$687M net cash position. EBIT margins swung from negative 31% to positive 32%, and the Los Gatos district, acquired in January 2025, is now the lowest-cost part of the portfolio. Our November 2024 buy call returned +171%.

The problem is the price. I built a mine-by-mine net asset value model using the company's own 2026 guidance and its December 2025 reserve statement, and it values the business at US$10.49 per share at spot prices. At US$17.42, the market is effectively assuming something like US$86/oz silver forever, or mine lives and a Jerritt Canyon outcome well beyond anything currently disclosed. Maybe that turns out right. Silver did touch all-time highs this year, and the Navidad discovery is a real one. But at 1.66x NAV you are paying today for drill results that don't exist yet. I re-initiate at Neutral with a US$15.75 target, which is 1.5x NAV, the top of the historical range for quality producers. I would get interested again near US$13 (about 1.25x spot NAV), or on news that actually moves NAV.

Scorecard first: what we got right and wrong in 2024

This note follows up a November 2024 analysis whose call and reasoning I graded in public, including the misses. The biggest one: we modeled the Gatos acquisition as debt-funded, but it was an all-stock deal, so the real cost was about 190M new shares of dilution rather than leverage. The full scorecard is on the track record page. One lesson carried directly into this note. Our 2024 model implied 6% upside and the stock returned 171%, because a peer-multiples model can't see the commodity price. So this time the valuation is a NAV model with an explicit silver price deck that you can argue with.

The one-line version of this report: the current share price equals my NAV at roughly $86/oz silver held forever, a 41% premium to spot. Owning AG here is a leveraged bet that the 2025-26 silver repricing is structural rather than cyclical. That might be right. It is not a value case.

Breaking down the premium: what $17.42 pays for

  • Base NAV at spot: $10.49. The mines, the cash, today's prices.
  • Jerritt Canyon beyond my placeholder (up to ~$1-2): my $2.31/share assumes 120k oz/yr. The resource base (4.1M oz M&I) could support more. The Q4 2026 study could also disappoint.
  • Mine-life extension (~$1-2): each extra year of reserve replacement across the Mexican portfolio adds roughly $0.40 to $0.60 per share. San Dimas has replaced reserves for years, and Navidad (29.7M AgEq oz of new inferred resources at Santa Elena) is the most likely source of upside here.
  • The silver deck (everything else): whatever is left is a price view. At $70 flat, NAV is about $13. The premium only closes above $85.

Catalysts (12 months)

  • Q4 2026: Jerritt Canyon pre-feasibility study (Stantec). Turns $2.31/share of placeholder into a real number, up or down. The single biggest NAV event on the calendar.
  • H2 2026 to 2027: Navidad drilling and the 2026 reserve statement. Resource-to-reserve conversion at Santa Elena directly extends the shortest-dated part of my NAV.
  • Ongoing: cost delivery. 2026 AISC guidance of $26.15 to $27.91/AgEq oz is up sharply from $21.17 in 2025, mostly price-linked royalties and duties. Beating it would be the cheapest source of upside.
  • H2 2027: Jerritt Canyon first production.

Risks to my view

  • Upside risk: silver itself. Another physical-shortage squeeze like early 2026 (silver above $70) would make a Neutral rating look slow. This is the main way I end up wrong.
  • Mexico fiscal and permitting risk: about 80% of NAV sits in Mexico, and mining duties are price-linked and have gone up before.
  • Cost inflation: the move from $21 to $27 AISC partly reflects structurally higher royalty loads at high prices. Margin leverage to silver is thinner than the 2024 story assumed.
  • Execution at Jerritt Canyon: the mine was suspended in 2023 for good reasons, and restarts of high-cost Nevada assets have a mixed record across the industry.
  • Dilution habit: 302M shares became 489M in two years. Accretive at the right price, but the next deal may not be.

Rating rationale and triggers

Neutral, 12-month target US$15.75, which is 1.5x my spot-deck NAV. That multiple is the top of the 0.8x to 1.5x range quality silver producers have historically commanded. I think First Majestic earns the top of the range: net cash balance sheet, the Los Gatos cost position, First Mint optionality, and a real track record of reserve replacement. I would upgrade to Buy below about US$13 (roughly 1.25x NAV), on a positive Jerritt Canyon study, or on reserve additions that meaningfully extend the Mexican mine lives. I would downgrade above about US$19 without NAV-accretive news, or if the Jerritt Canyon study comes in below about 100k oz/yr at more than about $2,100 AISC.

This target and rating are dated July 2, 2026 and get tracked publicly on the dashboard, the same way the November 2024 call was graded.

Appendix: model and assumptions

  • Mine-level DCF, 10-year horizon, mid-year convention, 5% discount rate (at 8%, NAV falls to $9.44).
  • Production and AISC per mine come from the company's January 2026 guidance. Mine lives are December 31, 2025 P&P reserves divided by guided production, plus conversion extensions of +3 (San Dimas), +2 (Santa Elena), +3 (Los Gatos) and +1 (La Encantada) years.
  • Price decks: base is $61 Ag / $4,120 Au flat (July 2, 2026 spot). The conservative and bull decks bracket published bank forecasts, which ranged from HSBC at $57/$49 for 2027/28 up to JPM and Goldman at $85 to $100.
  • Jerritt Canyon: 120k oz Au/yr from 2028, $1,900/oz AISC, $75M restart capex per the company's plan. This is a placeholder until the Q4 2026 pre-feasibility study. Excluding it entirely, NAV is $8.18/share.
  • Excluded: expansionary capex (guided at $154M to $171M for 2026) and the exploration value that capex creates, First Mint, and working-capital swings. These lean in offsetting directions but are known simplifications.
  • The model lives in Excel, fed by an automated market-data pipeline I set up with AI coding tools. Methodology and the graded 2024 call are on the project site.
