Investment thesis

First Majestic is a dramatically better business than the one we recommended in November 2024: record production (31.1M AgEq oz in 2025), a transformed balance sheet (US$687M net cash at Q1 2026 versus 2.1x debt/EBITDA eighteen months ago), EBIT margins that swung from −31% to +32%, and the successfully integrated Los Gatos district now contributing its lowest-cost ounces. Our November 2024 buy call returned +171%.

But price is what you pay, and today's price already capitalizes most of that transformation — and then some. Our mine-by-mine net-asset-value model, built on the company's own 2026 guidance and December 2025 reserves, values the business at US$10.49 per share at spot prices. At US$17.42, the market is either underwriting roughly US$86/oz silver in perpetuity, or assuming mine lives, exploration success, and a Jerritt Canyon outcome well beyond what is currently disclosed. Neither is impossible — silver touched all-time highs this year and the Navidad discovery is genuinely exciting — but at 1.66x NAV we are being asked to pay today for tomorrow's drill results. We re-initiate at Neutral with a US$15.75 target (1.5x NAV, the top of the historical quality-producer range, reflecting the net-cash balance sheet, cost position at Los Gatos, and mint optionality). We would turn buyers near US$13 (~1.25x spot NAV) or on fundamental news that moves NAV itself.

Scorecard first: what we got right and wrong in 2024

This report follows a November 2024 analysis whose call and reasoning we graded publicly — including the misses (we modeled the Gatos deal as debt-funded; it was all-stock, and the cost was ~190M new shares of dilution, not leverage). The full graded scorecard is on the track-record page. The relevant lesson carried into this note: our 2024 multiples model implied +6% upside and the stock returned +171% — because peer-relative multiples cannot see the commodity price. This report is therefore built on an explicit NAV model with a silver price deck you can argue with.

The one-line version of this report: the current share price equals our NAV at roughly $86/oz silver held forever — a 41% premium to spot. Owning AG here is a leveraged bet that the 2025–26 silver repricing is structural, not cyclical. That may prove right; it is not a value case.

Decomposing the premium — what $17.42 pays for

  • Base NAV at spot: $10.49. The mines, the cash, today's prices.
  • Jerritt Canyon beyond our placeholder (+ up to ~$1–2): our $2.31/share assumes 120k oz/yr; the resource base (4.1M oz M&I) could support more — or the Q4 2026 study could disappoint.
  • Mine-life extension (+ ~$1–2): each extra year of reserve replacement across the Mexican portfolio adds roughly $0.4–0.6/share. San Dimas has replaced reserves for years; Navidad (29.7M AgEq oz of new inferred resources at Santa Elena) is the most likely source of upside here.
  • The silver deck (the rest): everything remaining is a price view. At $70 flat, NAV is $13; the premium only closes above $85.

Catalysts (12 months)

  • Q4 2026 — Jerritt Canyon pre-feasibility study (Stantec): converts $2.31/share of placeholder into a real number, up or down. The single biggest NAV event on the calendar.
  • H2 2026–2027 — Navidad drilling and 2026 reserve statement: resource-to-reserve conversion at Santa Elena directly extends the shortest-dated part of our NAV.
  • Ongoing — cost delivery: 2026 AISC guidance of $26.15–27.91/AgEq oz is up sharply from $21.17 in 2025 (price-linked royalties and duties). Beating it would be the cheapest source of upside.
  • H2 2027 — Jerritt Canyon first production.

Risks to our view

  • Upside risk — silver. A physical-shortage squeeze like early 2026 (silver > $70) makes a Neutral rating look slow. This is the main way we're wrong.
  • Mexico fiscal and permitting risk: ~80% of NAV is Mexican; mining duties are price-linked and have risen before.
  • Cost inflation: the $21 → $27 AISC trajectory partly reflects structurally higher royalty loads at high prices — margin leverage to silver is thinner than the 2024 story assumed.
  • Execution at Jerritt Canyon: the mine was suspended in 2023 for good reasons; restarts of high-cost Nevada assets have a mixed industry record.
  • Dilution habit: 302M → 489M shares in two years. Accretive at the right price — but the next deal may not be.

Rating rationale and triggers

Neutral, 12-month target US$15.75 = 1.5x our spot-deck NAV — the top of the 0.8–1.5x range quality silver producers have historically commanded, which we justify with the net-cash balance sheet, Los Gatos cost position, First Mint optionality, and management's demonstrated reserve replacement. We would upgrade to Buy below ~US$13 (≈1.25x NAV), on a positive Jerritt Canyon study, or on reserve additions that materially extend Mexican mine lives. We would downgrade above ~US$19 absent NAV-accretive news, or on a Jerritt Canyon study that guides below ~100k oz/yr at above ~$2,100 AISC.

This target and rating are dated July 2, 2026 and will be tracked publicly on the dashboard, the same way our November 2024 call was graded.

Appendix: model and assumptions

  • Mine-level DCF, 10-year horizon, mid-year convention, 5% discount rate (sensitivity: 8% → NAV $9.44).
  • Production and AISC per mine: company 2026 guidance (Jan 2026). Mine lives: Dec 31 2025 P&P reserves ÷ guided production, plus conversion extensions of +3 (San Dimas), +2 (Santa Elena), +3 (Los Gatos), +1 (La Encantada) years.
  • Price decks: base $61 Ag / $4,120 Au flat (Jul 2 2026 spot); conservative and bull decks bracket published bank forecasts (HSBC $57/$49 for 2027/28 vs JPM/GS $85–100).
  • Jerritt Canyon: 120k oz Au/yr from 2028, $1,900/oz AISC, $75M restart capex per company plan — placeholder pending the Q4 2026 pre-feasibility study. Excluding it entirely: NAV $8.18/share.
  • Excluded: expansionary capex (guided $154–171M in 2026) and the exploration value it creates; First Mint; working-capital swings. These roughly offset in direction but are a known simplification.
  • Model built in Excel with an automated market-data pipeline; full methodology and the graded 2024 call at the project site.
