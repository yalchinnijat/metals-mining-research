# The November 2024 First Majestic Call — Graded

**Original analysis:** MGT 231 group project (Chris, Daha, Yalchin), University of Toronto Mississauga, dated November 21, 2024.
**Conclusion at the time:** First Majestic Silver (TSX: AG.TO / NYSE: AG) was undervalued at C$9.15 per share — a buying opportunity.
**Graded:** July 2, 2026 (19.5 months later).

---

## The headline

| | Nov 21, 2024 | Jul 2, 2026 | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| AG share price (NYSE, US$) | ~$6.54 | $16.89 | **+158%** |
| Silver spot (US$/oz) | ~$31 | ~$61 | **+97%** |
| AG 52-week range since | — | $7.74 – $32.03 | peaked ~+390% |

**The buy call was right — and then some.** But an honest grading has to separate what we predicted correctly from what we got right for the wrong reasons, and what we simply couldn't see.

---

## Prediction-by-prediction scorecard

### 1. "First Majestic is undervalued; this is a buying opportunity" — ✅ RIGHT (direction), ❌ magnitude
Our relative-valuation model (average of P/B, P/NAV, P/CF vs. peers) produced an intrinsic value of ~C$9.74 vs. a C$9.15 price — implying only ~6% upside. The stock returned ~158% (and at its peak, ~390%). **Lesson:** multiples-based valuation anchored to peer averages can tell you *cheap vs. peers*, but it structurally cannot capture the biggest driver of a miner's value — the commodity price itself. This is exactly why the professional standard in mining is a NAV model with an explicit silver price deck.

### 2. "The Gatos Silver acquisition will strain liquidity short-term, then debt-to-EBITDA improves as EBITDA scales" — 🟡 HALF RIGHT (right outcome, wrong mechanism)
We modeled the deal as a cash/debt-funded strain on the quick ratio. In reality the deal closed January 16, 2025 as an **all-stock** transaction — up to 190 million new shares issued (pro forma: 62% existing / 38% Gatos holders). So the "cost" wasn't liquidity, it was **dilution** — a mechanism our analysis didn't consider. The outcome side was right: Los Gatos contributed ~$490M revenue and ~$151M net earnings in 2025, integration delivered procurement synergies, and leverage ratios improved as EBITDA surged. **Lesson:** always check the deal's *consideration structure* before modeling its balance-sheet impact.

### 3. "First Majestic will break away from peers on AISC via economies of scale" — 🟡 PARTIAL
Consolidated AISC was $21.17/AgEq oz in 2025, essentially flat vs. $21.11 in 2024. Holding costs flat while integrating a major acquisition is respectable, but "break away from the peer group" is not yet demonstrated. Needs the refreshed peer comps (Phase 2) for a definitive grade.

### 4. "Jerritt Canyon will be sold or reopened as a more efficient mine, 2026–2027" — ✅ RIGHT — our best call
In April 2026, First Majestic announced a formal restart plan: $75M invested in 2026, pre-feasibility study (Stantec) due Q4 2026, production targeted for **H2 2027**, on a resource base of 4.1Moz M&I gold. Our slide literally said "Jerritt Canyon Mine (2026–2027)." Timeline nailed.

### 5. "First Mint vertical integration will boost margins" — ✅ RIGHT (direction), but still small
First Mint commenced bullion sales March 2024, earned ISO 9001 certification in April 2025 (making products IRA-eligible), and grew Q4 2025 revenue ~150% YoY to $22.7M. Real and growing — but still roughly 2% of company revenue, so the margin impact at the consolidated level is modest so far.

### 6. "Rising silver demand from green technologies supports long-term prices" — ✅ RIGHT (direction), different driver
Silver roughly doubled, hitting all-time highs before correcting below $60 in June 2026. But the proximate driver of the 2025–26 spike was a **physical shortage colliding with a Chinese retail-buying wave** — a demand-squeeze story, with industrial/solar demand as the structural backdrop rather than the trigger. **Lesson:** the thesis can be right while the catalyst comes from somewhere you weren't looking.

### 7. "ROE will improve on profitability, asset utilization, and portfolio streamlining" — ✅ RIGHT
From net loss of $182M (2023, ROE ~ -10%) to record profitability: Q1 2026 alone delivered $477M revenue (+95% YoY), $128M net earnings, $223M free cash flow, and the largest dividend in company history. The DuPont decomposition correctly identified net margin as the broken lever — and it's the lever that fixed itself when prices rose and Gatos scale arrived.

## What we'd do differently (and are doing, in the re-initiation)

1. **Build a NAV model with an explicit silver price deck** — the single biggest value driver was the one our model held constant.
2. **Grade against peers, not just the market.** Fresnillo returned ~500% over the past year — in a sector-wide rally, picking the *right silver miner* matters as much as being bullish silver. (Exact peer-relative returns from Nov 21, 2024 to be computed in Phase 2.)
3. **Model deal structure explicitly** — dilution vs. debt changes everything about per-share value.
4. **Separate "cheap vs. peers" from "cheap vs. intrinsic value"** — our multiples said +6%; the market said +158%. Both numbers were "true"; they answer different questions.
