## The trap questions

- *"Why Neutral after being right?"* → "Being right at 0.9x book in 2024 and disciplined at 1.66x NAV in
  2026 is the same skill: price versus value. The rating changed because the price did."
- *"What would make you wrong?"* → Silver squeeze past $70 (upside), or the Jerritt study surprising big.
  Both triggers are pre-committed in the note — I don't get to move the goalposts later.
- *"Isn't your model too simple?"* → "Yes, deliberately — flat production, AISC as all-in cost, 10-year cap;
  every simplification is listed in the appendix with its direction of bias. A model I can defend line-by-line
  beats a black box."
- *"Why is your NAV so far below price — is the model broken?"* → "Producers usually trade above a
  reserves-only NAV; the question is how far above. 1.66x with a $2.31 placeholder inside it is the rich end,
  and I decomposed exactly what fills the gap: Jerritt upside, life extension, and a price view."
